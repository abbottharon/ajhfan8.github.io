The Evolution of E-Commerce in Qatar: Customer Perceptions and Trends

Abstract

In recent years, the world has witnessed a remarkable surge in the popularity of e-commerce, with countries across the globe embracing online retail as a fundamental component of modern commerce. Qatar, although a relatively small player in the global e-commerce arena, is experiencing substantial growth in its online retail sector. However, Qatar still lags behind many other countries in terms of online purchasing penetration. This article explores the perceptions of Qatari consumers regarding the e-commerce purchasing process and the factors that influence these perceptions, both positively and negatively. While there is a growing demand for online shopping in Qatar, concerns about the safety of online payment processing persist.

Introduction

The business environment in Qatar is characterized by fierce competition, marked by a surge in online transactions and the proliferation of various forms of e-commerce offered by both local and international companies. Electronic Commerce (e-commerce) encompasses a broad spectrum of activities, including the exchange of business information, physical and digital goods and services, and transactions conducted through electronic means. These electronic channels encompass the internet, electronic data interchange (EDI) for transmitting purchase orders to suppliers, telephone and fax-based transactions, ATM-based payments, wireless networks, and smart cards for digital cash. E-commerce represents a shift from traditional brick-and-mortar business environments to a cyber space where transactions occur electronically rather than in physical spaces [1].

The adoption and implementation of e-commerce practices can yield significant benefits for both organizations and consumers. Organizations can reduce operational expenses, enhance overall efficiency, and streamline their business processes through e-commerce adoption. Meanwhile, consumers can save time and money through online purchasing and payment options offered by e-commerce entities. However, concerns related to online shopping and payment security have deterred some individuals from fully embracing e-commerce.

In the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) region, the e-commerce industry has seen rapid growth, with Qatar playing a noteworthy role. According to Visa International, the GCC e-commerce industry was projected to grow by 35% year-on-year to reach USD 15 billion in 2015, making it the world's fastest-growing e-commerce market [2]. Although online purchasing in Qatar has historically had one of the lowest penetration rates globally, it is expected to grow significantly, with projections estimating e-commerce in Qatar to reach $8 billion by 2018 [3]. The country's economic prosperity and global outreach have aligned it with prevailing global business trends.

This article aims to investigate consumer perceptions of e-commerce in Qatar, examining its economic impact as a marketing tool. Qatar, a relatively young Arab nation, has one of the world's highest per capita incomes, making it an intriguing subject for this case study.

Literature Review

The globalization, deregulation, and rapid advancement of information technology have blurred industry boundaries across various sectors. E-commerce offers numerous benefits, including increased product variety, cost savings, and time efficiency for consumers [4]. The adoption of online shopping has gained traction domestically, though cross-border e-commerce remains less prevalent. This fragmentation can lead to missed opportunities for consumers in terms of cost savings and product variety.

In Qatar, the e-commerce sector began to take shape in 2009. With a population estimated at 2.2 million, including a substantial expatriate majority, Qatar has demonstrated a strong appetite for luxury goods, with high per capita income supporting this trend. Several companies, such as Tejari.com, Souq.com, BurjMall.com, and Brownbag.ae, have tailored their approaches to cater to online consumers. The percentage of online shoppers in Qatar rose from 29% in 2009 to 42% in 2010 [5].

Siddiqui identifies ten interrelated factors influencing online shopping behavior in Qatar, including external environmental factors, individual characteristics, demographics, product features, website quality, attitudes toward online shopping, intention to shop online, decision-making processes, online purchasing behaviors, and customer satisfaction [5].

External environmental factors encompass circumstances that significantly impact consumers' online shopping behavior, including legal frameworks that protect online buyers from losses, third-party trust factors that ensure vendor honesty, and the number of competing organizations.

Demographics reveal that online shopping in Qatar is primarily driven by individuals aged 25 to 44. Young women in Qatar have increasingly turned to the internet for shopping. Personal characteristics encompass factors such as consumers' internet literacy, cultural influences, trust levels, engagement with products, and willingness to share information with others.

Product features refer to the characteristics of online stores, the range of products they offer, and the support services provided during transactions. Website quality encompasses aspects such as website content, presentation, search capabilities, navigation, technical features, security, and media richness.

Online shopping attitudes are critical as they influence consumers' intentions and decisions to shop online. Intention to shop online reflects consumers' inclination to make online purchases, including repeated ones that reflect customer loyalty. The decision to shop online involves activities such as information gathering, product comparisons, and choice-making. Online purchasing relates to placing orders and making payments online.

Customer satisfaction is a crucial metric, as it measures the extent to which consumers' expectations are met during the online shopping experience. Satisfied customers are more likely to develop positive attitudes and remain loyal to online shopping platforms.

A significant shift is observed in consumer behavior in Qatar, with a growing preference for mobile phones over personal computers for online shopping. Approximately 29% of Qatar's internet users have made purchases using smartphones, compared to 26% who use personal computers. In response to increasing online transactions, QNB bank in Qatar has partnered with Visa to enhance online security with the Verified by Visa system, introducing the Double Secure service.

Clothing emerges as the most popular product category for online shopping in Qatar. The introduction of e-commerce has driven continuous sales growth in the country. As more consumers seek the convenience and cost savings offered by online shopping, businesses are adjusting their strategies to meet this demand.

E-Commerce Customer Perceptions in Qatar

To conduct this research, an appropriate research methodology was employed. Research methodology can be broadly classified into qualitative and quantitative methods, each offering distinct advantages and disadvantages. Qualitative research delves into inner feelings, emotions, attitudes, gestures, thoughts, and behaviors of individuals related to the research theme [6]. In contrast, quantitative research involves the analysis and evaluation of data and information. In this study, qualitative research was employed to explore the feelings, opinions, thoughts, behaviors, and attitudes of consumers, given the exploratory nature of the research.

Qualitative research can take various orientations, including theory construction, explanatory, and language learning approaches. For this study, the language learning approach was chosen to provide meaning and context to words and expressions used by respondents. This approach allows for a detailed description and interpretation of social phenomena, aligning with the study's objectives.

Data analysis is a critical aspect of any research endeavor. Data can be collected through primary or secondary sources. Primary data collection involves obtaining raw data directly from the field, while secondary data collection relies on existing databases. Each method has its advantages and disadvantages. Primary data collection yields up-to-date information but can be costly and time-consuming. In contrast, secondary data collection is cost-effective and efficient but may lack validation and correctness.

In this study, a combination of primary and secondary data sources was used. Primary data was collected through open-ended questionnaires and interviews from a sample of 50 individuals. Thirty-seven respondents reported positive experiences with online shopping, citing benefits such as time and cost savings. However, 13 respondents expressed dissatisfaction, primarily due to concerns about online payment security. Two respondents had negative perceptions of e-commerce.

Overall, respondents emphasized the importance of effective quality control in online marketing practices, as well as the need for skilled technological professionals to address technical issues and glitches in online business operations. Ensuring the security of personal information during online payments was another paramount concern raised by all respondents.

In terms of economic stability, all 50 respondents believed that e-commerce purchasing could help individuals maintain economic stability. They noted that organizations adopted online business operations due to market demand and that cost reductions achieved through e-commerce allowed for competitive pricing.

Despite the growth in e-commerce, some respondents expressed concerns about the quality of service and security of personal information. Organizations were urged to prioritize these issues to cultivate customer trust.

Conclusion

Qatar is a rapidly developing nation with a young, dynamic, and globally oriented population. The country's robust economic growth has attracted numerous organizations seeking to establish a presence in Qatar's emerging market. Demand for online marketing activities is surging among Qatari consumers, with the trend expected to reduce the appeal of traditional brick-and-mortar retail.

This research demonstrates that e-commerce purchasing has gained significant traction in Qatar, driven by the country's economic prosperity and growing market demand. However, concerns about online security, particularly regarding personal information and account details, persist among consumers. Organizations must prioritize data security and quality control to maintain their brand image in Qatar's competitive market.

As Qatar continues its journey toward becoming a prominent player in the global e-commerce landscape, it is crucial for organizations to address these concerns effectively. By doing so, they can position themselves to leverage the full potential of e-commerce, cater to the evolving preferences of Qatari consumers, and contribute to the continued growth of the country's e-commerce sector.
